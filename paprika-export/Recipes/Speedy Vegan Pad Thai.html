Speedy Vegan Pad Thai

Total Time: 30 min Servings: 3 servings Source: kitchenful.com

Ingredients

2 tbsp olive oil

10 oz extra firm tofu

2 tsp olive oil

4 clove garlic

1 shallot

2 green onion

0.5 red chili pepper

4 oz rice noodles

1 carrot

1 red bell pepper

0.5 cup green cabbage

2.66 tbsp soy sauce

2 tbsp tamarind paste

1.33 tbsp granulated sugar

2 tsp rice wine vinegar

1 tbsp sriracha

0.5 lime

0.5 cup bean sprouts

Directions

Finely chop the shallot, green onion, garlic, and chili. Cut the tofu into cubes, shred the cabbage, and cut the red bell pepper and carrot into thin strips.

Add 1 tbsp olive oil to a medium pan over high heat. Once the olive oil is hot, add the tofu and cook until lightly crispy on all sides. Stir occasionally to avoid burning. When crispy, remove from the heat and set aside in a bowl.

Return the same pan to the stove on medium heat. Add 1 tbsp olive oil. When hot, add the garlic, shallots, green onions, and chili. Cook for 5-8 minutes, or until everything is lightly golden. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

Add the rice noodles to a large bowl and pour over some boiling water. Let sit to soften, and drain when 2 minutes away from being fully cooked. Rinse under cold water.

Add the carrot, bell pepper, and cabbage to the pan and cook for 5 minutes.
Stir in the soy sauce, tamarind paste, sugar, vinegar, sriracha, and lime juice.

Add the rice noodles to the pan, and toss to combine. Cook until warmed through and the noodles are fully cooked. Add the bean sprouts and tofu, and toss to coat. Top with desired garnish, and serve.
